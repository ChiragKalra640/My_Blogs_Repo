TAXES IN SHARE MARKET

  1. LTCG (LONG TERM CAPITAL GAIN)
  2. STCG (SHORT TERM CAPITAL GAIN)
  3. TAX ON BUSINESS INCOME

EXPLANATION OF TAXES

LONG TERM:- Long Term in stock market is holding share|stock for more than 1 year . Tax applied in This case is 10% of total profit . for example if you earn 100 rupees then, you have to pay 10 rupees as tax in case of LTCG

SHORT TERM:- Short Term in stock market is holding share for less than 1 year ,even if you hold for 1 day , or for 1 week or for month , you are most welcome in the bracket of STCG , and you have to pay 15% of your profit in taxes

TAX ON BUSINESS INCOME:- If stock market is the only place from where you earn money, or if you do "INTRADAY TRADING, F&O , COMMODITY , SHORT SELLING, SWING TRADES , POSITION TRADES ETC ETC " , all the things which stock market allows , then you are not an invester , because you are doing business then you have to pay tax according to your income tax slab , for example if you earn 10 crores then you have to pay 42% tax or accordingly
