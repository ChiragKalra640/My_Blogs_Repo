2) TYPES OF TRENDS

  1. UP-TREND :-

    • it is the collection of up moves and down moves
    • uptrend is the evidence by creation of higher highs and higher lows ( Higher highs and higher lows)
    • thumb rule : NEVER SELL IN UP-TREND!

  2. DOWN-TREND :-

    • it is the colllection of down moves and up moves
    • downtrend is evidence by creation of lower highs and lower lows (lower highs and lower lows)

  3. SIDEWAYS :-

    • price is consolidating within a range
    • downtrend is evdence by prices moving within a range
    • thumb rule:- NEVER TRADE IN A SIDEWAYS TREND . WAIT AND WATCH FOR THE DIRECTION OF BREAKOUT
    • SPECIAL TIP:- CALL OPTIONS CAN BE SOLD IN A SIDEWAYS TREND AS TIME VALUE IS GOING DOWN

  4. TREND REVERSALS :-

    • End of any trend and start of new another trend (starting of uptrend to downtrend or vice a versa is called trend reversal

3) SUPPORT AND RESISTANCE

  1. SUPPORT :-

    SUPPORT level is a price level where the price tends to find support as it is going down.

  2. RESISTANCE :-

    RESISTANCE level is a price level where the price tends to find resistance as it is going up.

  3. IMPORTANCE OF SUPPORT AND RESISTANCE
    • SUPPORT AND RESISTANCE analysis is an important part of trends because it can be used to make trading decisions and identity when treand is reversing.
    • A Resitance once broken becomes support and a support once broken becomes a Resitance.

4) BREAKOUT AND VOLUMES

Breakout with increased volumes is a better conformation for a change in the trend (from sideways to uptrend OR from sideways to downtrend)

Volumes provide us with the intensity of a given price moves

PIOTROSKI SCORE

  1. The PIOTROSKI SCORE is a ranking between zero and nine that incorporates nine factors that speak to a firm's finacial strength.
  2. It is named for JOSEPH PIOTROSKI a CHIKAGO ACCOUNTING PROFESSOR who created the scale , based on certain aspects of a corporation's FINANCIAL statements.
  3. The nine aspects are based on accounting results over a number of years , a point is awarded each time a standard is met , resulting in an overall score .
  4. The PIOTROSKI score is a favorite metric used to judge VALUE STOCKS.
  5. If a company has a score of 8 or 9 , it is considered a good value . If a comapny has a score of between 0 and 2 points , it is likely not a good value.

UNDERSTANDING THE PIOTROSKI SCORE

The PIOTROSKI score is broken down into the following category:-

  1. PROFITABILITY
  2. LEVERAGE, LIQUIDITY AND SOURCES OF FUNDS
  3. OPERATING EFFICIENCY

EXPLANATION PART:-

(PROFITABILITY CRITERIA INCLUDES)
  1. POSITIVE NET INCOME (1 POINT).
  2. POSITIVE RETURN ON ASSETS (ROA) in current year (1 POINT).
  3. POSITIVE OPERATING CASH FLOW in current year (1 POINT) .
  4. CASH FLOW FROM OPERATIONS BEING GREATER THEN NET INCOME (QUALITY OF EARNING)(1 POINT).
(LEVERAGE, LIQUIDITY AND SOURCES OF FUNDS CRITERIA INCLUDES)
  1. LOWER AMOUNT OF LONG TERM DEBT IN THE CURRENT PERIOD , COMPARED TO THE PREVIOUS YEAR (DECREASED LEVERAGE)(1 POINT).
  2. HIGHER CURRENT RATIO THIS YEAR COMPARED TO THE PREVIOUS YEAR (MORE LIQUIDITY)(1 POINT).
  3. NO NEW SHARES WERE ISSUED IN THE LAST YEAR (LACK OF DILUTION) (1 POINT).
(OPERATING EFFICIENCY CRITERIA INCLUDES)
  1. A HIGHER GROSS MARGINS COMPARED TO THE PREVIOUS YEAR(1 POINT).
  2. A HIGHER ASSET TURNOVER RATIO COMPARED TO THE PREVIOUS YEAR(1 POINT).
